What is Phishing Attack?

A phishing email attack is a fraudulent and deceptive email sent by cybercriminals who impersonate trusted entities or individuals to trick recipients into divulging sensitive information like passwords, financial details, or personal data, or into clicking on malicious links or attachments. The ultimate goal is to compromise the victim's security and privacy. Phishing attacks are prevalent in the digital realm and require vigilance and education to prevent falling for them.

❮ ❯

How do we prevent this?

To protect against phishing email attacks, individuals and organizations should implement a multi-faceted approach. This includes educating users to recognize phishing signs, using email filtering and security software to detect and block suspicious emails, verifying the authenticity of email senders, and avoiding clicking on unfamiliar links or downloading attachments from unknown sources. Two-factor authentication (2FA) should be enabled wherever possible to add an extra layer of security. Regularly updating software and keeping systems and antivirus programs current is also crucial in thwarting phishing attempts.
